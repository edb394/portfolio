Cave 420

Inspiration from Dunhuang

After visiting the Mogao Caves in Dunhuang, Gansu, China, I was inspired by the many different kinds of Buddhist drawings and paintings inscribed inside them. One of the most telling signs of a painting’s meaning is the mudra in which the depicted Buddha’s hands are performing. To get a better glimpse at this, I decided to make 3D models of one important mudra from the 420th cave in Mogao. The goal of this project was to spread an unknown aspect of cultural heritage in a format that could easily be integrated as just one piece of the puzzle in a vast knowledge base. By creating a wireframe UX design resulting in an app clip that can be opened within seconds, incredibly complex knowledge becomes accessible to many.

Cultural preservation & research

Perfecting the models

After studying the different mudras that can be found within Chinese Buddhist cave paintings, their history, and their meanings, I chose the dhyana mudra to represent here. After making a photogrammetry scan of a hand in the correct position I used various software to adjust it as necessary.
